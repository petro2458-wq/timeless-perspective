Account Rules
Starting Drawdown
The total simulated funds before account failure. On a $50K account with $2,500 drawdown, this is $2,500. The account label is irrelevant — only this number matters for sizing.
Profit Target (to pass)
How much profit you need to pass the evaluation or trigger a payout. This is your finish line.
Drawdown Type
EOD: floor updates once daily — most forgiving. Intraday: updates tick-by-tick on unrealized peaks — most punishing. Static: floor never moves.
Losing Trade Trailing DD Impact (R)
With intraday trailing drawdown, winners have no extra floor impact — the floor moves by exactly your closed profit. Losers DO impact the floor because price often moves in your favor before failing. Example: a trade goes +0.5R in your favor before reversing to a -1R loss. That is a 1.5R trailing drawdown impact. The next 2R winner completely erases this. Set this to your average favorable excursion on losing trades.
Daily Loss Limit
A per-day loss cap that stops trading for the day — but does NOT end the challenge. Account survives, resets next morning. Used by Tradeify Growth, Topstep, and others.
Consistency Rule
Your best single day must be ≤ this % of total profit at payout. A huge early win forces continued trading until that day is diluted below the threshold. Enter the % your firm uses (e.g. 35 for Tradeify, 50 for Topstep, 30 for Apex).
Max best-day %
Enter your firm's consistency rule %. Tradeify = 35. Topstep = 50. Apex = 30. Your best single day profit must stay below this % of total profits to qualify for payout.
Your System
Win Rate %
% of trades that close as winners. Calculate from your last 50+ trades. Most traders overestimate this.
Breakeven %
Scratch trades — close flat or near zero. Loss rate = 100% − Win% − Breakeven%.
R-Multiple (avg win / avg loss)
If you risk $200 and your avg win = $400, R = 2.0. Your average loss is always 1R by definition. R is your unit of measure.
